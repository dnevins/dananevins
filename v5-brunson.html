The Story

The year was 1998. I was standing in a dusty driveway in Southern California, covered in grease, trying to track down a quarter panel for a 1970 Ford Bronco I had no business restoring.

That’s when I started buying parts from Jeff’s Bronco Graveyard — one of the early pioneers of selling auto parts on the internet. The websites were ugly. The search was broken. Checkout was a nightmare. But people were buying anyway, because they had no other choice.

I saw the gap before I had a name for it. The automotive aftermarket was going digital whether it was ready or not, and nobody was building the technology these businesses actually needed.

In 2000, I founded Web Shop Manager with a simple thesis: parts retailers deserve ecommerce tools as sophisticated as their product data. Fitment matters. Year-make-model lookup matters. ACES and PIES compliance matters. Nobody else was solving for that.

Twenty-five years later, that thesis has scaled into a platform powering over a thousand businesses — auto parts, truck accessories, powersports, marine, firearms, and more. We’ve processed north of a billion dollars in transactions. We’ve been recognized by Inc. 5000, won three W3 Gold Awards, and earned a Best Place to Work designation.

In 2020, I started PartsLogic because the industry’s needs were evolving again. Agencies and developers needed modern, API-first tools for building automotive ecommerce experiences. Smart search. Intelligent fitment. Better data infrastructure.

Every morning, I wake up working on the same problem that grabbed me in that driveway twenty-five years ago: how do you make it radically simple to sell complex parts online?
