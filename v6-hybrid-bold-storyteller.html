How a Broken Bronco Led to a Billion-Dollar Platform

In 1998, I was standing in a driveway in Southern California, trying to source a quarter panel for a 1970 Ford Bronco I had no business restoring. I started buying parts from Jeff's Bronco Graveyard — one of the very first aftermarket businesses selling online.

The websites were ugly. Search was broken. Checkout was a nightmare. But people were buying anyway, because they had no choice. I saw the gap before I had a name for it.

The automotive aftermarket was going digital whether it was ready or not. And nobody was building technology that understood how this industry actually works — fitment data, year-make-model lookup, ACES and PIES compliance, complex catalog structures with thousands of SKUs.

In 2000, I founded Web Shop Manager. The thesis was simple: parts retailers deserve ecommerce tools as sophisticated as their product data.

That thesis scaled. Over the next two decades, Web Shop Manager became the aftermarket's most trusted ecommerce platform — powering over a thousand stores across auto, truck, powersports, marine, firearms, and more. We crossed a billion dollars in processed transactions. Inc. 5000 took notice. We won three W3 Gold Awards. SEMA invited me to speak year after year.

In 2020, I saw the next gap. Agencies and developers needed modern, API-first tools for building automotive ecommerce. So I started PartsLogic — smart search, intelligent fitment, better data infrastructure.

Twenty-five years in, I'm still solving the same problem that grabbed me in that driveway: how do you make it radically simple to sell complex parts online?
